RevOps Pipeline Flow

01 Capture
📡
Traffic sources
Meta Ads / Outbound
Top of funnel

Every lead begins here. Meta Ads and outbound channels drive traffic into the capture layer. Each source must be UTM-tagged at the campaign, ad set, and ad level so attribution does not break downstream.

  • Meta Ads (Facebook and Instagram) targeting your ICP with UTM parameters on every link
  • Outbound via cold email or LinkedIn feeding directly into GHL with source tagged
  • Missing UTMs at this layer is the first place Meta attribution breaks
  • Conversions API (CAPI) must be active alongside the Meta Pixel post-iOS 14
🎯
Lead capture layer
Lovable (Forms / Pages)
Intake

The face of the stack. Landing pages and forms built in Lovable convert ad traffic into form submissions. The moment a form is submitted, a webhook fires and the automation chain begins.

  • Custom landing pages per campaign or audience segment
  • Forms capture contact info, intent, and qualifying responses
  • Meta Pixel fires on the thank-you page to register a lead event
  • Webhook endpoint must be updated after every Lovable redeploy or leads go into a black hole
Critical risk: if the webhook URL goes stale after a Lovable redeploy, Make never receives the submission. No alert fires. Leads disappear silently.
02 Enrich
⚙️
Ingestion and normalization
Make (connective tissue, not the brain)
Critical

Make is the nervous system, not the brain. Its job is three things: receive Lovable webhooks and standardise the payload, run deduplication before anything hits GHL, and fire bidirectional sync events when deals close. Keep each scenario single-purpose.

  • Deduplication: checks for existing contacts by email before creating a new record
  • Field mapping: normalises form responses into the exact custom field names GHL expects
  • Source attribution: stamps each contact with campaign, ad set, ad, and offer at creation time
  • On deal close, fires a Meta CAPI conversion event back to the ad platform
  • Every scenario must have error handling. Scenarios that silently fail on edge cases are the same as no scenario
High risk: UTM data captured in the URL but not mapped to GHL custom fields means no ROI attribution and no source context for sales. This is one of the most common and invisible failures in this stack type.
🗂️
CRM (single source of truth)
GoHighLevel (GHL)
Core

GHL is the brain. Every platform feeds GHL. GHL never feeds another system as a secondary record. If it is not in GHL, it did not happen. The contact record created here carries the full context every rep needs before their first call.

  • Lead source and campaign (which Meta ad, which offer)
  • Form responses (what they said they need)
  • Full engagement history (every email open, link click, page visit)
  • Lead score updated dynamically on each engagement event
  • Audit check: custom fields populated consistently, no orphaned workflows, no duplicate contacts with the same email on different IDs
03 Nurture
✉️
Nurture sequence
GHL Workflow / Automations
Marketing owns

The moment a contact is created in GHL, the workflow fires. Lead enters the pipeline at "Raw Lead" and the nurture sequence begins immediately. Every engagement event updates the lead score and moves the contact closer to the MQL threshold.

  • Immediate auto-reply via SMS and email on form submission
  • Staged nurture sequence runs until the lead engages or exits
  • Lead score increments on email opens, link clicks, replies, and page visits
  • SLA timers and escalation alerts enforce response time commitments
  • If unresponsive after 3 sales attempts: return to nurture, do not discard the lead
High risk: duplicate automations built by different people responding to the same trigger. A lead receiving three emails in ten minutes is a sign of overlapping workflows. Audit before adding new ones.

05 Handoff
📤
Marketing delivers
Context-complete handoff

Marketing owns the lead until the MQL threshold is hit. At handoff, the contact record must be complete.

  • All custom fields mapped: source, campaign, offer, form responses
  • Lead score at or above the agreed threshold before pushing to sales
  • At least one meaningful engagement logged (open, click, or reply)
  • Nurture sequence paused at handoff. Sales owns all comms from here.
  • Slack or GHL notification sent to assigned rep with a context summary
📞
Sales commits
SLA-enforced contact

Sales receives full context. A lead contacted within 5 minutes is 9x more likely to convert than one contacted after 30. The SLA timer starts at handoff.

  • First contact attempt within 5 minutes of handoff notification
  • Review the full activity timeline before dialling. No "how did you hear about us" questions.
  • Stage updated in GHL within 30 minutes of contact attempt
  • All call notes logged to the contact record. Nothing lives in memory or notebooks.
  • After 3 failed attempts: return lead to nurture workflow, do not discard
06 Close
💼
Pipeline (GHL Deals)
Contacted → Qualified → Proposal → Closed
Deals

The active deal board where every opportunity is tracked in real time. Stage movement here is what closes the attribution loop back to Meta.

  • Contacted: first outreach made, SLA timer started, stage set to SQL / In Contact
  • Qualified: confirmed fit after discovery, sales owns the conversation fully
  • Proposal: offer or pricing sent, deal record active in GHL
  • Closed Won: deal signed, revenue attributed, Make fires reporting update
  • Closed Lost: outcome recorded with a loss reason. Loss reasons feed directly into marketing insights.
07 Feedback Loop
🔁
Closed-loop attribution
Revenue back to Meta via Make and CAPI
Critical

Without this layer, you are flying blind on what is actually working. Meta needs to know what happened to the leads it sent. CAPI conversion events from closed deals train the algorithm to find better prospects. This is non-negotiable.

  • When a deal closes in GHL, Make fires a Meta CAPI conversion event immediately
  • Revenue is attributed back to the originating campaign, ad set, and ad
  • Loss reasons from GHL become marketing insights that improve targeting and messaging
  • Stage-by-stage conversion data surfaces which lifecycle stage is leaking leads
  • Can you tie closed revenue to a specific Meta campaign? If not, this layer is missing or broken.
Make's role here: when a deal closes in GHL, Make pushes a conversion event back to Meta for campaign optimisation. This is the highest-ROI automation in the entire stack and the one most commonly skipped.
